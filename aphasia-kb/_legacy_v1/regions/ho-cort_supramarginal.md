# Supramarginal Gyrus

## Anatomical context

Inferior parietal lobule, surrounding the posterior end of the Sylvian
fissure. Together with the angular gyrus, it forms part of the dorsal
language stream. Anatomically near the cortical termination of the arcuate
fasciculus.

## Lesion-symptom evidence

Lesions centered on left SMG are classically associated with conduction
aphasia: fluent output, preserved comprehension, but striking impairment of
repetition with frequent phonemic paraphasias. The Geschwind 1965
disconnection account remains the most-cited theoretical framing.

## Therapy implications

Therapies focused on phonological encoding (e.g., phonological component
analysis) are commonly used. Evidence for region-specific therapy targeting
is limited and warrants clinician judgment.
