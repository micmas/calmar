# Superior Temporal Gyrus, posterior division (Wernicke's area, core)

## Anatomical context

Posterior left superior temporal gyrus, the canonical location of Wernicke's
area in classical models. Sits dorsal to the superior temporal sulcus, with
direct connections to angular gyrus and supramarginal gyrus. Receives the
posterior terminations of the arcuate fasciculus.

## Lesion-symptom evidence

Damage to pSTG produces the prototypical fluent receptive aphasia: largely
preserved articulation and prosody, frequent neologisms and semantic
paraphasias, and impaired single-word and sentence comprehension. Lesion-
symptom mapping consistently localizes auditory comprehension deficits to
this region (Fridriksson 2018; Mirman 2015).

## Therapy implications

Comprehension-focused therapies (e.g., Treatment of Underlying Forms,
auditory comprehension drills) are typically prioritized over output-focused
therapies for this lesion pattern. MIT is generally not indicated.

## Cautions

- Hemispheric asymmetry: bilateral pSTG damage is required for permanent
  word-deafness; unilateral pSTG damage usually allows partial recovery of
  comprehension.
