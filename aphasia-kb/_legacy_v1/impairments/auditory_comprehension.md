# Auditory comprehension

Impaired auditory comprehension is the cardinal feature of fluent
posterior aphasias (Wernicke's, transcortical sensory). It can range from
single-word level (when severe) to sentence- and discourse-level only (when
mild).

## Lesion correlates

Posterior left STG is the most consistent predictor. Damage extending into
the middle temporal gyrus and angular gyrus increases the severity of
comprehension impairment, and bilateral pSTG damage is required for
permanent word-deafness.

## Recovery and therapy

Comprehension-focused therapies (auditory drills, Treatment of Underlying
Forms for syntactic comprehension) are typically prioritized. There is
limited region-specific therapy-response evidence in this domain.
