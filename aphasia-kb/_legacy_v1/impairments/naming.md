# Naming / word retrieval

Anomia is the most common chronic symptom of aphasia and occurs across
nearly all subtypes. The *type* of naming error (semantic vs phonological)
is more diagnostic of lesion location than the presence of anomia itself.

## Lesion correlates

- **Semantic errors** (saying "dog" for "cat"): more strongly associated with
  anterior temporal lobe and anterior IFG lesions.
- **Phonological errors** (saying "tat" for "cat"): more strongly associated
  with posterior dorsal-stream lesions (SMG, pSTG, AF).

## Recovery and therapy

Semantic Feature Analysis (SFA) is the most-studied therapy for anomia and
shows benefit across aphasia subtypes; see `therapies/sfa.md`. Phonological
Component Analysis is an analogous option when phonological errors dominate.
