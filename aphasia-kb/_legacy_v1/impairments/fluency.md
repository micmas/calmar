# Speech fluency

Reduced fluency is the cardinal feature of non-fluent / Broca's-type
aphasia. It encompasses reduced phrase length, effortful articulation,
agrammatism, and impaired prosody.

## Lesion correlates

Predictors are concentrated in the left frontal operculum (pars opercularis
in particular), with secondary contributions from anterior IFG, ventral
premotor cortex, anterior insula, and the underlying white matter of the
arcuate fasciculus.

Lesions confined to posterior temporal cortex (pSTG) typically **preserve**
fluency, producing the contrasting fluent paraphasic pattern of Wernicke's
aphasia.

## Recovery and therapy

Output-focused therapies (MIT, CIAT, script training) are typically
prioritized. See `therapies/mit.md` and `therapies/ciat.md`.
