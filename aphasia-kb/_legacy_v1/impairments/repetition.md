# Repetition

Disproportionately impaired repetition with relatively spared fluency and
comprehension is the hallmark of conduction aphasia. Geschwind's
disconnection account locates the lesion at or near the SMG and the
overlying arcuate fasciculus.

## Lesion correlates

The classical pattern requires either cortical SMG damage or sub-cortical
arcuate fasciculus damage (or both). Pure cortical lesions of pars
opercularis can produce repetition deficits when arcuate involvement is also
present.

## Recovery and therapy

Phonological Component Analysis and direct repetition drills are commonly
used. Right-hemisphere arcuate integrity is associated with better recovery
prognosis (Schlaug 2008; secondary inference).
