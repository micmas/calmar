# Melodic Intonation Therapy (MIT)

## What it is

Patients are taught to intone short utterances (sung on two pitches) while
tapping their left hand on each syllable. Therapy progresses through stages
of decreasing clinician support, with the goal of carry-over to spoken
propositional speech.

## Evidence base

- Schlaug 2008 reported neural and behavioral changes in non-fluent aphasia,
  including increased right-hemisphere arcuate fibers after intensive MIT.
- Subsequent RCTs show mixed effect sizes; benefit is most consistent in
  patients with chronic non-fluent aphasia and intact right-hemisphere
  white matter.

## Who is it for

Patients with non-fluent aphasia after a left-hemisphere stroke,
particularly with anterior dominant lesions and at least partial
preservation of comprehension. Less well-suited to fluent posterior
aphasias.
