# Semantic Feature Analysis (SFA)

## What it is

A structured therapy in which the patient is shown a picture of a target
item and prompted (with clinician support) to generate semantic features:
*category*, *use/function*, *action*, *properties*, *location*, and
*association*. The cumulative semantic activation supports lexical
retrieval.

## Evidence base

- Boyle 2004 demonstrated treatment-related gains in two patients with
  fluent aphasia.
- Subsequent group studies have replicated benefit across aphasia subtypes,
  with maintenance and generalization to untreated items in many cases.

## Who is it for

Any patient with anomia as a primary presenting feature, irrespective of
classical subtype. Region-level "responder profiles" are weak in the
literature; the strongest predictor of response remains baseline severity
and engagement, not lesion location.
