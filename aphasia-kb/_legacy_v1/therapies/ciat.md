# Constraint-Induced Aphasia Therapy (CIAT)

## What it is

Adapted from constraint-induced movement therapy. The patient is constrained
to speech as the only communicative channel during intensive structured
group games (e.g., card-request games), with massed practice over a short
period.

## Evidence base

- Pulvermüller 2001 demonstrated significant gains in chronic aphasia after
  10 days of CIAT, contrasted against conventional therapy of equal total
  duration.
- Subsequent studies confirm benefit but with variable effect sizes;
  intensity, dose, and patient stamina are key moderators.

## Who is it for

Chronic aphasia with at least minimal verbal output and the stamina to
tolerate a high-intensity schedule. Less well-suited to acute presentations
or patients with severe global aphasia who cannot participate in the
required exchanges.
