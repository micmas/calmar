# Communicative Shaping

## RTSS specification

**Target**: Verbal output complexity; naturalness of communicative exchange.

**Ingredient**: Progressive increase in the communicative demands placed on the patient within structured card-exchange or role-play games: clinician raises the bar from single words to phrases to full sentences, rewarding successive approximations toward more complex verbal output.

**Theory**: Operant shaping via successive approximation: reinforcing incrementally closer approximations to the target behaviour shifts the patient's verbal output distribution upward over time without provoking failure-driven withdrawal.

## Used in therapies

- constraint_induced_aphasia_therapy
- melodic_intonation_therapy


## Addresses impairments

- brocas_aphasia
- anomic_aphasia
- fluency


## Notes

In MIT, shaping is operationalised as the four-stage fading protocol (unison intoning → fading → immediate repetition → delayed repetition → spontaneous production). In CIAT, it is implemented by the clinician raising lexical and syntactic complexity requirements within card-game interactions.
