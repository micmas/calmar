# Inhibitory rTMS (Right Pars Triangularis / BA45)

## RTSS specification

**Target**: Interhemispheric competition; transcallosal inhibition from right IFG to left perilesional cortex.

**Ingredient**: 1 Hz slow repetitive TMS applied over right pars triangularis (BA45) for 10 consecutive days (~20 min/session), using a figure-of-eight coil positioned via neuronavigation or 10-20 EEG landmarks. No concurrent behavioural treatment in the original Naeser 2005 protocol.

**Theory**: Naeser interhemispheric competition model: after left-hemisphere stroke, right IFG pars triangularis can become overactive and exert excessive transcallosal inhibition on left perilesional language cortex. Inhibitory (1 Hz) rTMS suppresses right IFG firing, reducing this transcallosal inhibition and permitting greater perilesional cortex recruitment for language.

## Used in therapies

- tms_right_ifg__Naeser2005


## Addresses impairments

- anomic_aphasia
- brocas_aphasia
- fluency


## Notes

Evidence base is small (Naeser 2005, open pilot, n=4). Response depends on extent of right hemisphere compensation; patients with primarily right-hemisphere language may be harmed. Requires neuronavigation for precision. Not suitable for patients with seizure history, metal implants, or pacemakers.
