# Listen–Repeat–Spell–Copy–Spell Cycle

## RTSS specification

**Target**: Orthographic word form retrieval; graphemic buffer; phonological-to-orthographic conversion.

**Ingredient**: For each target word in a 40-item individualised list, the patient: (1) listens to the word spoken by the clinician; (2) repeats the word aloud; (3) attempts to spell it; (4) copies the correct spelling; (5) re-spells from memory. The cycle emphasises the auditory-phonological input step before each spelling attempt.

**Theory**: Multimodal encoding: the listen-repeat step activates phonological representations of the word, providing a strong phonological scaffold for subsequent graphemic retrieval. The copy-and-resell sequence combines errorless encoding with immediate retrieval practice, strengthening phonological-to-orthographic conversion pathways. The listen-repeat component may also improve phonological processing per se (Barbieri 2023: RH aSTG upregulation possibly reflecting improved phoneme discrimination).

## Used in therapies

- spell_study_spell


## Addresses impairments

- dysgraphia


## Notes

The listen-repeat step is operationally important: Barbieri 2023 attributes unexpected right-hemisphere aSTG upregulation in the spelling group to phonological-discrimination improvements from this component, rather than to spelling-specific neural changes.
