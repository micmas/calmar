# Verb Argument Structure Training

## RTSS specification

**Target**: Thematic role assignment; syntactic processing; sentence production and comprehension.

**Ingredient**: Meta-linguistic steps using written verb and noun cards: (1) identify the verb and its arguments from an action picture; (2) assign Agent and Theme thematic roles; (3) construct an active sentence; (4) clinician demonstrates passive derivation (NP-movement); (5) patient produces the passive independently. Training focuses on non-canonical structures (passives, object-extracted relatives, wh-questions) that require syntactic movement operations.

**Theory**: Complexity Account of Treatment Efficacy (CATE; Thompson & Shapiro 2005): training complex non-canonical structures generalises to simpler canonical ones because complex structures subsume the operations required for simpler ones. Restores access to thematic mapping procedures and the verb-argument network that supports sentence-level language use. Barbieri et al. 2023 shows TUF induces right-hemisphere upregulation that correlates with verb and sentence comprehension improvement, suggesting right-hemisphere syntactic network recruitment.
