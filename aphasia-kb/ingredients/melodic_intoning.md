# Melodic Intoning

## RTSS specification

**Target**: Articulatory planning; speech output; right-hemisphere language homologue recruitment.

**Ingredient**: Target utterances are sung on two pitches (a minor third apart, e.g., E–G) with exaggerated prosodic contour, progressing through four stages of decreasing clinician support: unison intoning → clinician fading → immediate patient repetition → delayed repetition → spontaneous production.

**Theory**: Rhythmic-melodic scaffolding reduces demands on the damaged left premotor/Broca area by offloading articulatory sequencing to right-hemisphere prosodic and motor networks (right STG, right premotor). Intensive MIT is associated with increased right arcuate fasciculus volume (Schlaug 2008), consistent with structural neuroplasticity in the right hemisphere pathway.

## Used in therapies

- melodic_intonation_therapy


## Addresses impairments

- brocas_aphasia
- apraxia_of_speech
- fluency
- form_to_articulation


## Notes

Patient must have at least minimal voluntary phonation to participate. Contra-indicated for Wernicke's aphasia with fluent paraphasic output and for patients with significant hearing impairment.
