Nadeau & Crosson (1997) critically review the literature on subcortical aphasia across
48 pages. Their principal contribution is a two-pronged theoretical synthesis: (1)
nonthalamic subcortical aphasias (basal ganglia and capsular lesions) arise mainly from
associated cortical hypoperfusion rather than direct striatal language mechanisms; and
(2) thalamic aphasia reflects disruption of a frontal–ITP–NR–CM selective engagement
circuit that gates lexical–semantic cortical networks. The review also proposes that
thalamic systems are selectively involved in declarative (but not procedural) cognitive
processes including lexical retrieval, working memory, and episodic memory encoding.
