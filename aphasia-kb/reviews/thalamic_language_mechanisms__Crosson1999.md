Crosson (1999) reviews four thalamic aphasia cases (BD, WT, RD, BC) to test and extend
the selective engagement model proposed in Nadeau & Crosson (1997). The central synthesis
is that the frontal-ITP-NR-CM system selectively gates the semantic-lexical output
interface, making semantically-driven naming selectively vulnerable while sparing more
automatic phonological tasks. Posterior thalamic (pulvinar) lesions produce different and
more variable deficits that depend on the cortical connectivity of the affected thalamic
region, including visual reading errors and category-specific anomia.
